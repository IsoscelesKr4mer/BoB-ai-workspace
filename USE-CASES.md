# Use Cases

Real workflows, with example prompts you can use or adapt. Organized roughly from simple to complex.

---

## Daily Ops

### Morning briefing
Start the day knowing what's in front of you — upcoming meetings, overdue action items, renewal flags, anything that came in overnight.

> *"Give me my morning briefing."*

The AI will check today's calendar, scan `tracking/to-do.md` and `tracking/waiting-on.md` for overdue items, flag any renewal dates inside 30 days, and surface recent customer emails that need a reply. Good first message of the day.

### End-of-day wrap
Before you close your laptop, check what got done and what's still open.

> *"End of day wrap — what's still open from today and what do I need to hit tomorrow?"*

### Weekly review
Zoom out. What needs attention this week that isn't already on your radar?

> *"Weekly review — what accounts haven't I touched in the last 2 weeks, what renewals are coming up in the next 60 days, and what's overdue in my waiting-on list?"*

## Email

### Draft a reply with account context
You get an email from a customer asking about something. Instead of replying from memory, let the AI pull the account file, check open items, and draft a reply that actually references what's been discussed.

> *"Draft a reply to Sarah's email about the data export issue. Pull context from the Acme account file first."*

### Draft a reply from an existing thread
Paste a thread or give the AI a subject line to search for. It reads the whole conversation, then drafts a reply that fits the tone and picks up where things left off.

> *"Search my inbox for the thread with Marcus at Bridgeton about the renewal and draft a reply moving it forward. Check the account file first."*

### Follow-up after a meeting
You just finished a call. Hit it while it's fresh.

> *"Draft a follow-up to today's call with FanDuel. Pull the action items from the meeting notes and include them."*

### Renewal outreach — 90 days out
Don't wait for the renewal to sneak up. Get ahead of it.

> *"Draft a renewal kickoff email to [contact] at [account]. Their renewal is in 83 days. Keep it casual — we have a good relationship. Don't make it sound like a contract alert."*

### Proactive usage check-in
An account's usage has been declining. Reach out before they bring it up.

> *"Draft a check-in email to [contact] at [account]. Mixpanel shows their clip volume dropped about 40% over the last 30 days. Don't lead with the data — open with checking in, then mention we noticed some lighter activity and want to make sure they're getting what they need."*

### Executive escalation
Something went wrong and you need to get ahead of it with the executive sponsor.

> *"Draft an escalation email to [VP name] at [account] re: the outage last Tuesday. Factual, direct, no finger-pointing. Include what happened, what we've done, and what we're doing to prevent it."*

### Referral or case study ask
When an account is healthy and engaged, this is often the right move.

> *"Draft a case study ask email to [contact] at [account]. They've been with us 2 years and usage has been growing. Keep it short, make it easy to say yes."*

### Inbox triage
You have 40 unread emails. Figure out what actually needs a response.

> *"Scan my inbox and give me a prioritized list of what needs a reply. Flag anything from customers first."*

Then: *"Draft replies for items 1, 3, and 5."*

## Call Prep

### Standard check-in prep
The bread and butter. Account context, open items, usage, renewal status, suggested agenda — one briefing before you dial.

> *"Prep me for my call with Acme at 2pm."*

### QBR prep
Deeper than a check-in. Includes usage trends over time, ROI narrative, strategic context.

> *"Prep me for Acme's QBR. Pull usage data for the last 12 months and pull recent news on the company. I want to show them the trend and come in with an expansion angle."*

### Renewal negotiation prep
Going into a renewal with a customer who's been quiet or showed hesitation.

> *"Prep me for Bridgeton's renewal call. They pushed back on price last quarter. Pull their usage, check if we have any open issues, and give me a positioning angle for holding on ARR."*

### Escalation call prep
Something blew up. You're getting on a call with a customer who isn't happy.

> *"Prep me for an escalation call with [contact] at [account]. They emailed yesterday about [issue]. I need the full account history, open items, and a clear picture of what we owe them."*

### Executive sponsor intro
You're being introduced to the exec sponsor for the first time.

> *"Prep me for an intro call with [VP name] at [account]. Pull what I know about the account and any news about the company or the VP. Help me come in with something specific to reference."*

## Meeting Notes

### Process raw notes
Paste whatever you wrote — shorthand, fragments, incomplete sentences. The AI will structure it.

> *"Here are my notes from the Acme call today: [paste notes]"*

### Pull and process from Notion
The meeting was recorded or transcribed in Notion. One sentence to file it all.

> *"Grab my notes from today's FanDuel meeting in Notion and process them."*

### Process a voice transcription
Exported a transcript from Otter, Fireflies, Fathom, or Zoom? Paste it.

> *"Process this transcript from today's call with Bridgeton: [paste transcript]"*

## Account Intelligence

### Full account snapshot
Before a renewal, a new team member takes over an account, or you just want a clean current view.

> *"Give me a full current snapshot of the Acme account — contract, contacts, usage trend, open items, and any risk signals from recent notes."*

### Competitive intel
A customer mentions a competitor during a call. Do some quick research before your next touchpoint.

> *"[Account] mentioned they're also evaluating [Competitor]. Pull any intel you can find on [Competitor] and give me a quick comparison to what we offer."*

### Company news scan
Significant news at a customer's company often changes the relationship dynamics.

> *"Search for news on [Account] from the last 90 days. Flag anything that could affect the renewal or the relationship."*

### Contact research
You're about to email someone you've never met.

> *"Look up [name] at [account] — LinkedIn summary, role context, anything in my notes. I'm emailing them cold."*

## Portfolio & Analytics

### Portfolio health check
See where your risk is distributed across all accounts at once.

> *"Run a health check across my whole portfolio. I want to see renewal risk, usage trend, and open items by account."*

### Usage trend report
Where is engagement growing? Where is it eroding?

> *"Pull usage data for all accounts — clips and sessions, last 30 days vs last 90 days. Show me the trend for each account and flag anything I should act on."*

### Cross-reference: declining usage + upcoming renewal
The highest-risk combination in CS. Find it before the customer brings it up.

> *"Which of my accounts have both a renewal in the next 90 days and declining usage in the last 30 days? Rank by risk."*

### Accounts you haven't touched in a while
Easy to let healthy accounts go quiet for too long.

> *"Which accounts haven't had a logged meeting or email in the last 45 days? Flag anyone with a renewal inside 180 days."*

### Accounts not fully using what they're paying for
Expansion opportunity or churn risk depending on how you look at it.

> *"Which of my accounts have the lowest usage relative to their ARR? I want to prioritize outreach to get them actually using the product."*

### Who's most likely to expand?
High usage + strong relationship + contract headroom = the expansion conversation.

> *"Look at usage trends and contract sizes across my portfolio. Which accounts are growing fastest and might be ready for an expansion conversation?"*

### Segment accounts by product
Useful for targeted outreach when there's a product update, a new feature, or a vertical play.

> *"Which of my accounts are on [Product X]? I want to send a targeted email about [feature/update]."*

## Renewals & Risk

### Renewal pipeline review
Stay on top of what's coming up and what needs to move.

> *"Give me a full renewal pipeline review — everything coming up in the next 180 days, sorted by date, with usage trend and open items for each."*

### Flag accounts at risk
Beyond just renewal dates — accounts showing behavioral signs of churn.

> *"Flag any accounts showing churn signals — declining usage, overdue open items, recent escalations, or no contact in 60+ days."*

### Draft a renewal QBR deck outline
Structure a QBR agenda around the data you have.

> *"Build a QBR outline for [account]. Include usage data for the past year, wins we can highlight, open items to resolve before the renewal, and a section on where we go next. I'll use this to build the actual deck."*

### Post-renewal thank you / kickoff
After a renewal closes, cement the relationship.

> *"Draft a post-renewal email to [contact] at [account]. We just closed. Thank them, set the tone for the next term, and mention one specific thing we want to work on together."*

## Knowledge Base

### File new information about an account
You learned something in a call, in an email, on LinkedIn. Get it into the account file before you forget.

> *"Add to the Acme account file: their head of communications is leaving next month, replacement TBD. Could affect the QBR timeline."*

### Update a contact record
> *"Update the Acme account file — Sarah got promoted to VP of Marketing last week."*

### Build an account file from scratch
New account lands in your portfolio. Get a baseline in the system.

> *"Create an account file for [Account]. Pull what you can from HubSpot and search for recent news on the company."*

### Build a process doc
Document a workflow you repeat so the AI (and you) can follow it consistently.

> *"I want to document my escalation process. Here's how I handle it: [describe]. Write this up as a skill file I can reuse."*

## Advanced / Cross-Tool Queries

### HubSpot sync + Mixpanel usage report in one pass
The full portfolio data refresh. Takes a few minutes but gives you a complete picture.

> *"Sync my portfolio from HubSpot, then run the Mixpanel usage report for all accounts. Cross-reference and show me where I have renewal risk + declining usage."*

### Pre-QBR data package
Everything you need to build a customer-facing QBR, pulled automatically.

> *"Build me a data package for the Acme QBR: HubSpot contract details, Mixpanel usage for the last 12 months broken down by quarter, any open items from our tracking files, and a web search for news from the past 6 months."*

### Weekly digest to a Slack or Discord channel
Automate your Monday morning portfolio summary to a team channel.

> *"Post a weekly portfolio digest to #general: renewal flags, accounts with declining usage, and my top 3 action items for the week."*

### Prep for a board or leadership update
Summarize your portfolio for a non-CS audience.

> *"I need to update my VP on portfolio health this week. Pull total ARR, renewal forecast for Q2, any at-risk accounts, and 2-3 wins from the last 30 days. Keep it tight — this is a 5-minute slot."*

### Identify the right moment to ask for a referral
Don't ask too early or too late.

> *"Look at my accounts and tell me which ones are in the best position for a referral ask right now — strong usage, long tenure, no open issues, recent positive interactions."*

### Build a success story from usage data
Turn usage numbers into a narrative your customer can share internally.

> *"[Account] is going up for budget review. Draft a 1-page success story they can share internally — what they were trying to solve, how they're using the product, and what the results look like. Use their Mixpanel data for the numbers."*
