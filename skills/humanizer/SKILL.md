# Humanizer

## When It Runs
Automatic — applied to any customer-facing prose before it's presented. This means emails, follow-ups, QBR content, meeting summaries sent to customers. It does NOT run on internal notes, AI responses, file confirmations, or memory files.

## The Kill List

**Phrases — remove or rewrite:**
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "Please don't hesitate to reach out"
- "Per our conversation" → reference what was specifically discussed
- "As discussed" → be specific
- "I wanted to follow up" → just follow up
- "Just circling back" → state the actual purpose
- "Hope you're doing well" → skip entirely or be specific and genuine
- "At your earliest convenience" → give a real date or timeframe
- Any phrase that could appear unchanged in a corporate email template

**Openers — never use:**
- "Certainly" / "Absolutely" / "Of course" / "Great question"
- "I'd be happy to" / "I'm excited to"

**Words — replace:**
- leverage → use / streamline → simplify / delve → dig / aligned → on the same page
- synergy → [cut it] / utilize → use / facilitate → help / optimize → improve
- robust / scalable / holistic / proactive → [cut or rewrite]

**Structures — avoid:**
- Em dashes (—) → use commas, periods, or parentheses instead
- Rhetorical rule-of-three lists used for effect
- Performed authenticity ("to be completely transparent with you...")
- Hedging stacks ("I think maybe we could potentially consider...")
- Excessive qualifiers

## Tone Target
Competent account owner who types fast and genuinely likes the people they work with. Direct, warm, specific. Not corporate. Not cold. There's a difference between not being fluffy and being cold — avoid both.

- Reference something specific to the situation rather than a generic opener
- It's fine to say you're glad something worked out, or that something sounds good (if you mean it)
- Match their energy: if they're casual, be casual; if they're stressed, be steady
- Short sentences fine. Contractions fine. Starting with "And" or "But" fine.

## Process
1. Read the full draft
2. Scan for every item in the kill list — especially em dashes
3. Rewrite any flagged phrase or sentence (don't just swap characters)
4. After fixing anything, scan the whole draft again
5. Present the cleaned draft
